# Three-Domain Positioning Playbook

Classify every target into one domain at intake. This file defines, per domain: how to recognize it, which evidence leads, which keywords to mirror, the core narrative, the resume master to start from, and the live risks. The cross-cutting AI-builder layer sits on top of all three.

## How to classify

Read the title + the JD's first third (responsibilities + must-haves):
- Owns roadmap, metrics, prioritization, GTM, P&L, stakeholder/eng alignment → **Domain 1 (PM/Business).**
- Owns screens, flows, prototypes, design systems, usability of *digital* products → **Domain 2 (Software Design).**
- Owns physical form, CMF, ergonomics/human factors, hardware, manufacturability, wearables → **Domain 3 (Physical Design).**
- Hybrid (e.g., "Product Designer, hardware-software") → pick the **primary** owner of the role and treat the other as the differentiator. Note where the applicant's background is unusually strong at the software↔physical seam; say so.

When ambiguous, ask the applicant one question: "Is this role primarily about the product *decision* (PM), the *digital* experience (software design), or the *physical* object (industrial design)?"

## Domain 1 — Product Management / Business

**Recognize:** Senior/Staff PM, Product Lead, Product Strategy, Group PM, biz-ops/strategy-adjacent product roles.

**Gating:** Title-gated. If "PM" isn't on the title history, the play is to *launder the title* — win a credentialing PM role to make the next hardware-software PM role uncontestable. A fintech/SaaS Senior PM is an acceptable stepping stone here (unlike in design, where fintech harms the pivot).

**Lead evidence (in rough priority):** Pull from `projects.md` — prioritize entries that demonstrate:
1. Business judgment at scale (large-org finance, strategy, or operations at a recognizable company).
2. MBA or equivalent business credentials.
3. 0→1 ownership: raised capital, led a team, owned P&L, navigated a pivot to positive revenue.
4. Operating + metrics track record: scaling initiatives, NPS programs, platform implementations.
5. Research → strategy: discovery feeding multi-year roadmaps.
6. AI-native product thinking: shipped products + agent orchestration (the 2026 PM differentiator).

**Mirror these keywords (only where true):** product roadmap, prioritization (RICE/JTBD), GTM, P&L, north-star metric, leading/lagging metrics, funnel, A/B testing, experimentation, discovery vs. delivery, stakeholder management, cross-functional leadership, 0→1, AI product sense, evals, cost-per-inference, latency/quality tradeoffs.

**Resume master:** PM master. Weight bullets ~**40% discovery / 40% delivery outcomes / 20% leadership**. Name *what kind* of PM the role wants (growth, platform, technical, B2B, consumer, AI) in the first five seconds.

**Core narrative:** "I've done the PM job under other titles — founder, strategist, VP of CX — at scale and 0→1 scale, and I now ship AI-native products weekly. Give me the title and I'll make it obvious in a quarter."

**Live risks:** title gap (use the reframe); founder-as-flight-risk (use the stock reframe).

## Domain 2 — Software / Digital Product Design

**Recognize:** Product Designer, Senior/Staff Product Designer, UX Designer, Interaction Designer, AI Experience Designer, Design Systems Designer.

**Gating:** Portfolio-gated. The portfolio and case studies decide it. **Avoid fintech *design* roles** — they pull against the hardware/wearable/AI-native pivot.

**Lead evidence (in rough priority):** Pull from `projects.md` — prioritize entries that demonstrate:
1. Shipped AI-native products — AI experience design across multiple surfaces. Shipped, not concepts.
2. Design-systems credential: foreground when the JD mentions scale/consistency/systems.
3. Journey maps, archetypes, communication strategy for a recognizable enterprise client; digital-experience strategy.
4. End-to-end product design on a real shipped wearable companion experience.
5. Web + mobile UI/UX across real clients.
6. AI experience design — designing *for* probabilistic/agentic systems; the differentiator in 2026 software-design loops.

**Mirror these keywords (only where true):** UX, UI, user research, usability testing, prototyping, interaction design, end-to-end ownership, design systems, design tokens, Figma, accessibility (WCAG), information architecture, responsive design, AI experience design, agentic UX, design QA.

**Resume master:** Software-Design master (fork of the existing Design master, digital emphasis). Lead with UX/UI, research, prototyping, end-to-end ownership; foreground design systems when scalability is named; foreground accessibility when required.

**Core narrative:** "I design and ship real digital products end-to-end — most recently AI-native apps in weeks — and I design *for* AI, not just with it."

**Live risks:** title-history breadth ("is this person a real designer or a strategist?") → answer with shipped surfaces and case studies, not adjectives.

## Domain 3 — Physical / Industrial Product Design

**Recognize:** Industrial Designer, Product Designer (hardware), Human Factors Engineer/Designer, Design Engineer, Wearables Designer, CMF Designer, UX for hardware/HMI.

**Gating:** Portfolio- *and* craft-gated. Sketching, prototyping, materials/manufacturing fluency, and human-factors rigor are all assessed. Be honest about where the applicant is IC-strong vs. pivoting.

**Lead evidence (in rough priority):** Pull from `projects.md` — prioritize entries that demonstrate:
1. Formal industrial design credential — the credential that opens this domain.
2. A hero hardware project with multiple working prototypes and human-factors validation — a pricing-sensitivity or desirability study, a usability instrument (e.g., USE questionnaire), a satisfaction metric (e.g., NPS) — and quantified outcomes. Real hardware, real testing, real results — and any recognition the project earned.
3. Ergonomic/anthropometric study: Humanscale, body percentiles, quantified population impact.
4. Cross-platform shipping that proves hardware-software seam capability.
5. Materials/manufacturing/sustainability fluency (TA, bio-inspired design, etc.).
6. Human-factors frameworks — IEC 62366, AAMI HE75, FDA Applying Human Factors. ⚠️ Frame as methodology the applicant will come up to speed on quickly (the *practice* — task analysis, anthropometrics, usability validation — is what matters), not as standards already certified in.

**Mirror these keywords (only where true):** industrial design, human factors, ergonomics, anthropometrics, Humanscale, task analysis, usability validation, rapid prototyping, CMF, design for manufacturing (DFM), Fusion 360, SolidWorks, Keyshot, hardware-software integration, wearables, sketching/ideation.

**Resume master:** Physical-Design master (fork of the Design master, ID/hardware/human-factors emphasis). Lead with the MID credential, hardware hero project, and the ergonomic study; foreground prototyping volume and quantified human-factors outcomes.

**Core narrative:** "Trained industrial designer who validates with real prototypes and real human-factors data, and who now bridges the physical object to its software — exactly where wearables and connected hardware live."

**Live risks:** pivoting-into-formal-human-factors ("step back to an IC foundation and earn the senior role rather than borrow it"); no specific industry experience (closest analog + fast methodology ramp).

## Cross-cutting: the AI-builder layer (all three domains)

The applicant builds *with* AI; they are not an engineer. Apply per domain:
- **PM:** AI product sense + vibe-coding is now a scored pillar in top loops (Meta, Google). Shipped products and agent orchestration are a *direct* asset — lean in hard.
- **Software design:** AI experience design + design-systems automation. Designing for agentic/probabilistic UX is the frontier skill.
- **Physical design:** AI as a prototyping/analysis accelerant and the software half of connected hardware.

Never bolt AI onto non-AI work. Never claim authorship of infrastructure code. Signal fluency through shipped surfaces, stacks, and architecture-level reasoning.

## Two-master vs. three-master note

The installed setup uses two masters (PM, Design). For clean three-domain support, **recommend forking the Design master into a Software-Design master and a Physical-Design master.** Until the applicant confirms, tailor physical-design targets from the Design master re-weighted toward ID/human-factors evidence, and flag that a dedicated Physical-Design master would tailor faster and cleaner.
