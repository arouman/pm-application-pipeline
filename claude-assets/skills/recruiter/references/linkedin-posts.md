# LinkedIn Posts (in the applicant's voice)

Posts do two jobs: keep the applicant's profile active (algorithm reward) and build a credible public point of view that makes recruiters and hiring managers want them. The bar is *earned attention*, not engagement-bait. Never post anything that contradicts the dossier or inflates a claim — a public overclaim is far more dangerous than a private one.

## The applicant's voice (match it exactly)

Read the applicant's cover letters and materials to capture the register. The voice to aim for is:
- **Short, confident, declarative.** Prose, not bullet soup. No padding, no hedging, no "I'm humbled to announce."
- **Leads with an idea or a reframe**, often a slightly philosophical hook ("Surgery is the highest-stakes user interface there is." / "Taking companies 0→1 is like moving a massive boulder.").
- **Concrete and specific** — real numbers, real artifacts, real tradeoffs. Earns claims; doesn't assert them.
- **Builder's bias** — bias toward shipping, toward "problem → solution → ship → iterate," toward doing over theorizing.
- **Intellectually honest** — names the risk or the gap directly rather than spinning. Vulnerability used as credibility.
- **Warm but not gushy.** No emoji-spray, no hashtag walls (1–3 precise hashtags max). No motivational-poster tone.

Avoid: corporate LinkedIn clichés ("thrilled," "honored," "game-changer," "synergy"), engagement-bait ("Agree? 👇"), and humble-brags. Avoid first-person-plural for solo work; the applicant can say "I directed the agents; we built it together" for an orchestration system they led, but not "we" for things they did alone.

## Post formats that fit this voice

1. **The build log.** What was shipped, the friction it solved, one honest tradeoff, what was learned. Ends on the insight, not a CTA.
2. **The reframe.** Takes a common assumption in design/PM/AI and flips it, backed by a concrete example from the applicant's work. Mirrors the cover-letter hook style.
3. **The teardown.** A short, fair critique of a product's experience or a process, with a "here's the principle" payoff. Shows product judgment.
4. **The lesson from the seam.** Hardware↔software, design↔business, founder↔IC — the boundaries the applicant actually lives on. This is differentiated territory.
5. **The honest pivot note.** Where the applicant is growing — vulnerability-as-credibility, never as apology.

## Content pillars (rotate)

- **Building with AI** — shipping AI-native products solo-fast, agent orchestration, what AI changes about the product/design loop. The strongest, most current pillar.
- **The hardware-software seam** — wearables, connected hardware, where physical and digital meet.
- **Design ↔ business** — tying design decisions to P&L and outcomes; the MBA + design combination.
- **0→1 and the founder's lens** — what ventures and accelerator experience taught.
- **Human-centered rigor** — research, human factors, measuring what you ship.

## Structure of a strong post

- **Line 1 is the whole game** — the hook must earn the "see more" click. Lead with the idea or the surprising specific, never with throat-clearing.
- **Tight body** — 3–8 short lines or a compact list; one idea, well made.
- **Land on the insight** — end with the principle or the open question, not a sales CTA.
- **1–3 precise hashtags**, no wall.
- **Length:** most posts 80–200 words; build logs can run longer if the detail earns it.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the pillar + format + the specific artifact/claim it's built on (must trace to the dossier).
2. Draft in the applicant's voice; show 1–2 variants if the angle is a real choice (e.g., "build log" vs. "reframe" of the same ship).
3. Run the Universal Guardrails — public claims get the *strictest* accuracy check (product tiers, AI attribution, scope claims, no line-of-code bragging, publication status of any named work).
4. Offer a one-line first-comment idea (links go in the first comment, not the body, to protect reach) and a posting-time note if asked.

## Guardrail

If a post would imply a capability, title, metric, or product state not in the dossier, stop and flag it. The applicant's public credibility is the asset; don't spend it on an unverified line.
