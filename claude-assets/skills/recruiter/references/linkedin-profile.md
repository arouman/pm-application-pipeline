# LinkedIn Profile

LinkedIn in 2026 is a ranked search document, not a static CV. Recruiters spend ~6 seconds on a profile; the algorithm pre-filters on keyword match, skills, recency, and engagement, and AI search increasingly ranks on semantic relevance (so depth matters, not just keyword presence). ~87–90% of recruiters source here. Optimize every text section; LinkedIn doesn't index images.

**Strategic fork:** the applicant's profile must serve **recruiter search for job-seeking**, not B2B selling. That means target-role keywords, ATS-aligned skills, and an explicit "what I'm looking for." Pick **one primary target domain** for the headline; cover the others in About. Don't try to rank for all three roles in the headline at once — it dilutes every search.

## Headline (≤220 characters — use most of them)

Formula: **Target Role | 2–3 searchable specializations | one credibility marker / value line.** Lead with the *role they want*, not just titles they've held. Separate with pipes.

Domain-primary templates (pick the active job search's domain):
- **PM/Business:** `Senior Product Manager | AI-Native Products · Hardware-Software | [Credential], [School] | Shipped [N] AI products in weeks`
- **Software Design:** `Senior Product Designer | AI Experience Design · Design Systems | Shipped [N] AI-native apps | [Background] | MID+MBA`
- **Physical Design:** `Industrial / Product Designer | Human Factors · Wearables · Hardware-Software | MID [School] | [Hero project or recognition] | [Background]`

Rules: include the exact title recruiters search; weave 4–5 key skills across headline + profile; keep it current (recency is weighted); optionally add "Open to [role]" but skip the green "#OpenToWork" banner on the photo (some hiring managers read it as desperation — use the recruiter-only setting instead).

## About (2,600 characters — applicant's voice, keyword-rich)

Four-paragraph arc, written in their short confident register (see `linkedin-posts.md` for voice):
1. **Hook** — one sharp line on the through-line of their work (the reframe, not "results-driven professional").
2. **What they do (value)** — expertise + specialization + top achievements with numbers; naturally weave 8–12 target keywords for the primary domain.
3. **Why they're different (differentiator)** — the rare combination the applicant brings (e.g., industrial design + MBA + ships AI-native products weekly + the hardware-software seam). Name the other two domains here so they surface for those searches too.
4. **What they're looking for (CTA)** — explicit target role(s), domains, relocation openness, and how to reach them.

Keep claims compliant with the dossier — no performance inflation; product claims must match `projects.md` accuracy flags.

## Experience section

- Mirror the resume's accomplishment bullets (XYZ, frontloaded metrics), lightly de-formalized.
- Each role's first line carries the context tag (`Domain · Scale · Marker`) so the 6-second scan lands.
- Keyword density matters here too — the algorithm reads experience descriptions, not just the headline.
- Put the shipped AI products as their own roles/projects with stacks and links, framed accurately.

## Skills, Featured, Recommendations, Education

- **Skills:** pin the 3 that match the primary domain's top recruiter searches; endorsements raise ranking for that skill. List a full 12–15 ATS-aligned skills across the three domains, primary-domain skills first.
- **Featured:** link the applicant's portfolio site, product sites, and any standout case study. Featured is prime real estate — use it as a portfolio shortcut.
- **Recommendations:** aim for ~1 per 3 years of experience; ask former managers/clients (not peers) and give them a 3-part template (project, outcome, one quality).
- **Education:** complete it fully (incomplete education hurts ranking); list all degrees and any relevant certifications you hold — recent skill certs (AI, cloud, domain-specific) boost search appearances.

## Activity

The algorithm rewards consistent activity — even one post/week meaningfully lifts profile visibility. Pair profile work with the cadence in `linkedin-posts.md`.

## Guardrails

Same dossier accuracy as everywhere. Don't let the looser LinkedIn register become an excuse to inflate scope, titles, or product claims. Run the Universal Guardrails before publishing any section.
